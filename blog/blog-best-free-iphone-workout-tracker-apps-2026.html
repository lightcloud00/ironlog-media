Best Free iPhone Workout Tracker Apps 2026

Published May 23, 2026 · 8 min read

Tracking your workouts is one of the simplest ways to actually make progress in the gym. Write it down, repeat it next week, add a little weight or a few more reps. That's the whole game. But the apps out there keep putting up walls — caps on routines, paywalls around your own history, accounts required just to open the app.

If you've been looking for something that just works and doesn't hold your data hostage, here's where to start.

2. Strong

Price: Free tier available | Account Required: Yes | Routines: 3 max (free) | History: Full (paid)

Strong is one of the most established names in the space. The free tier gets you basic logging, a built-in exercise library, and limited routines. The app itself is solid — the interface is clean and the set-entry flow is fast.

But the caps are real. Three routines isn't much if you run a push/pull/legs split plus an arms day. And if you want to see your full workout history, you need the paid version. At $4.99/month, you're paying to access your own data.

Good if: You want a proven app and don't mind the routine cap.

Watch out for: The paywall on history and the 3-routine limit.

3. Hevy

Price: Free tier available | Account Required: Yes | Routines: 4 max (free) | History: 3 months only (free)

Hevy has a modern feel and some genuinely nice touches — good exercise search, clean stats, and a solid library. The free tier bumps you up to 4 routines, which is slightly more generous than Strong.

But here's the catch that catches a lot of people: Hevy hides your workout history after 3 months on the free plan. If you've been training for six months and want to look back at a lift from January? Gone, unless you pay.

For serious lifters, that's a dealbreaker. Training progress is built on seeing where you've been. Taking that away behind a paywall feels backwards.

Good if: You train casually and won't miss old sessions.

Watch out for: The 3-month history cutoff on free.

4. Stronger

Price: Free | Account Required: Yes | Routines: Limited | History: Limited

Stronger is a simpler, less polished option. It handles the basics — log sets, track bodyweight — but the interface feels dated and the exercise library is thin compared to the others. Account is required even for basic use.

It's free, which is nice, but the experience doesn't feel like it was built by someone who actually lifts.

How We Chose

These apps were evaluated on:

  • History access — is your full log available without paying
  • Account requirements — can you start logging immediately?
  • Interface quality — does the app get in your way or help you move faster?
  • Data ownership — can you export your stuff if you want to leave?

IronLog was built specifically to solve the problems we kept running into with the other apps. We wanted the unlimited parts without the catches.
