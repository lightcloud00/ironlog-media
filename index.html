FAQ

Got Questions?

What happens if my app crashes mid-workout?

Nothing is lost. IronLog uses bulletproof autosave with atomic writes — every set is committed to storage within 300ms of being logged. If the app crashes, your phone dies, or you close the app by accident, the crash recovery system detects incomplete sessions on relaunch and restores your exact workout state: every exercise, every set, every rep and weight. A visual save indicator shows you in real-time when data is being written. Zero data loss is not a marketing claim — it's the architecture.

What exercises are included?

The current public release includes 100+ built-in exercises across 9 muscle groups, with quick search and one-tap add flows.

How is my data stored?

The current public release is local-first. Workout history is stored on-device, and you do not need an account to use the core tracker.

Is my data secure?

Workout data stays on your device by default, and IronLog does not require you to create an account for the current public release.

What tools are in the active codebase?

The live iPhone release focuses on exercise logging, workout history, stats, PR detection, and local-first storage. Broader experiments like extra platform support and optional progress-photo flows are still being reviewed before public rollout.

What platforms do you support?

The current public release is on the iPhone App Store. Android, web, and Apple Watch are not publicly shipped yet.

Do I need an account?

No. The current public app works without an account, so you can start logging workouts immediately.
